Apollo 9

Close

Apollo 9 mission, which carried for the first time all elements intended for landing on the moon, was essentially supposed to test all procedures implying lunar module. During 10 days, from March, 3rd of 1969, Saturn V flew on low Earth orbit, and the astronauts had to stow and detach lunar module, what they will have to do to land on the moon and go back to Earth. Apollo 9 confirmed the technology developped for the program was ready for the mission. Lunar module has been named Spider and the command module Gumdrop. Some extra vehicular exit were made by the astronauts to get sure that a lunar exist will be possible.

Apollo 12

Close

More ambitious despite the smaller budget compared to Apollo 11, Apollo 12 had as a goal to study moon's surface. 3 submissions were assigned to this missions. First was landing on the moon with accuracy to get the probe Surveyor 3, second wad to make for the first time videos of the moon in color, and the last was to deploy a complet set of scientific instrument ASEP : a seismometer, a magnetometer, a spectrometer to detect the composition of solar wind, a super-thermique ion detector, a cold cathodic gauge, and a dust detector. Two spacewalks were scheduled, and the way back to earth included some maneuvers.
Unfortunately, when Saturn V was launched, it has been hit by the lightning, which provoked a big loss of electric power. Astronauts finally achieved to fix it, and they have been able to keep doing the mission. During the mission, lots of photos were taken, 34.1kg of moon floor sample were taken, and the astronauts' excursions lasted 7h45 in total, going 470m far away the landing place. In this mission, a lot of modifications were brought to the lunar module compared to Apollo 11, particularly the accuracy of landing. The launch of this mission took place on November, 14th, 1969.

Spacecrafts

Close

NASA designed the Apollo Command Module for this program. It was a capsule with room for three astronauts. The astronauts rode in the Command Module on the way to the moon and back. It was larger than the spacecraft used in the Mercury and Gemini programs. The astronauts had room to move around inside the spacecraft. Another spacecraft, the Lunar Module, was used for landing on the moon. This spacecraft carried astronauts from orbit around the moon to the moon's surface, then back into orbit. It could carry two astronauts. Two types of rockets were used for the Apollo program. The first flights used the smaller Saturn I (1) B rocket. It was about as tall as a 22-story building. This rocket had two stages. When the first part ran out of fuel, it dropped away from the other and burned up in Earth's atmosphere. The second part continued flying. The Saturn IB rocket was used to test the new Apollo capsule in Earth orbit. The other flights used the more powerful Saturn V (5) rocket. This three-stage rocket sent the Apollo spacecraft to the moon. It was about as tall as a 36-story building.

Apollo 10

Close

Last mission before set foot on the moon, Apollo 10 consisted in a last repetition in testing ascending and descending lunar orbit for lunar module. It was the second time a spacecraft approached the moon, after Apollo 8. It was also a last checking of procedures between lunar module and command module. That's how, on May, 18th of 1969, Saturn V took off for the last mission before the ultimate objective.
Once Apollo 10 was in orbit around the moon, in circular orbit at 110km of altitude, Stafford and Cernan went into the lunar module to realize a moon landing simulation. Lunar module is composed of one descent floor and one rising floor. Descent last around one hour, and then begin ascendant phase. Lunar module is placed on a particular orbit, that will allow the meeting with the command module. The meeting went perfectly, and the spacecraft came back on Earth after around 192 hours of mission.

Apollo 8

Close

Apollo 8 is today in the shadow of Apollo 11, but it has been a real success and a revolution in the space race, given that it was :
• The first leaving of Earth orbit by men
• The first entire vision of Earth by men
• First orbit around another star (moon)
• First vision of moon's hidden side by men
• First vision of a Earth-rise by men.
It was the first time Saturn V was used. The launch took place on December, 21st of 1968.

Apollo 7

Close

Apollo 7 was the first mission of Apollo Program. Its aim was to test the new spacecraft that will be used for this program. It has been launch by the Saturn IB rocket. It was the first time that three men were sent to space, and also the first time some images were diffused on TV.
One of the main goal of this mission was to test the stowage procedure with the lunar module. Given Saturn IB didn't carry the lunar module, astronauts tested it with the upper floor of the rocket. They also tested the engine that would be supposed to allow to leave moon orbit for the next missions. The engine was switched on 8 times, and everything worked perfectly.
